# WOOD Token — Revenue-Driven Tokenomics

> **Status:** Design Spec
> **Author:** Ally (AI CEO)
> **Date:** 2026-04-06
> **History:** Replaced the ve(3,3) emission-driven model after comparative analysis showed 50%+ emission budgets cause death spirals for non-DEX protocols. Redesigned around real protocol fee revenue.

## Overview

A revenue-driven tokenomics system for the Sherwood protocol. Users lock WOOD tokens for veWOOD to earn a direct share of protocol fee revenue (paid in USDC). The protocol is controlled by a multisig — veWOOD is purely an economic mechanism for fee sharing, not governance. Per-syndicate `shareToken/WOOD` Aerodrome Slipstream pools provide secondary market exit for depositors during locked redemption periods. Time-limited bootstrapping incentives (15% of supply, 12 months, steep decay) replace the open-ended emission model. After month 12, the protocol operates entirely on real revenue — no further token emissions.

**Core thesis:** Token value comes from protocol fees generated by successful AI-managed strategies, not from inflationary emissions. If the protocol doesn't generate revenue, the token shouldn't have artificial value.

## Tokens

| Token | Standard | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| `$WOOD` | ERC-20 (LayerZero OFT) | Utility token — locked for fee sharing, traded. Natively bridgeable to any LZ-supported chain. Fixed supply minted at genesis. |
| `$veWOOD` | ERC-721 (veNFT) | Fee-sharing NFT — represents locked WOOD with time-weighted fee share weight. Earns pro-rata share of real protocol fee revenue. |

## The Flywheel

```
Lock WOOD → veWOOD → fee share weight
  ↓
Real fees from strategy performance → distributed to veWOOD holders in USDC
  ↓
More TVL from strategy success → more fees → higher veWOOD yield
  ↓
Higher yield → more people lock WOOD → less circulating supply → price support
  ↓
shareToken/WOOD pools provide exit liquidity → depositors feel safer → more TVL
  ↓
Buyback removes WOOD from market → locked as protocol veWOOD → further supply reduction
  ↓
Repeat — driven by REAL REVENUE, not emissions

BOOTSTRAPPING ACCELERATOR (months 1-12):
  Non-transferable WOOD rewards → lockIntoVeWOOD() → fee share weight WITHOUT sell pressure
  This bootstraps veWOOD supply from day 1: early participants earn fee share + accumulate
  governance weight, all before a single bootstrapping token hits the market (6-month lock)
```

**Key difference from v3:** The v3 flywheel depended on WOOD emissions being valuable (WOOD price ↑ → emissions more valuable → more deposits). This is circular — it only works if WOOD price goes up. This flywheel is grounded in real protocol fee revenue. If strategies perform well, fees are generated regardless of WOOD price. veWOOD holders earn USDC, not more WOOD.

## Detailed Design

### 1. Vote-Escrow Locking (VotingEscrow.sol)

Users lock WOOD for a chosen duration (4 weeks — 1 year) and receive a veWOOD NFT. The veWOOD balance determines the holder's share of protocol fee revenue — it is **not** a governance mechanism.

**Fee share weight scales linearly with lock duration:**
- 100 WOOD locked 1 year → 100 veWOOD fee weight
- 100 WOOD locked 6 months → 50 veWOOD fee weight
- 100 WOOD locked 4 weeks → ~7.69 veWOOD fee weight

**Minimum lock: 4 weeks.** Shorter locks allow mercenary capital to farm fee distributions with minimal commitment. A 4-week minimum ensures holders have meaningful skin in the game.

**Fee weight decays linearly** as the lock approaches expiry, incentivizing longer locks and re-locks.

**Auto-Max Lock:** Optional flag per veNFT — treated as 1-year lock with no decay. Can be toggled on/off.

**Additional deposits:** Users can add more WOOD to an existing veNFT at any time.

**Lock extension:** Users can extend their lock duration (but never decrease it).

**Why 1 year (not 4)?** Sherwood chose 1 year for three reasons: (1) faster capital rotation — syndicate strategies have shorter time horizons than DEX liquidity mining, (2) lower commitment barrier — fee sharing should be accessible, and (3) alignment with the 12-month bootstrapping period — the incentive program and the maximum lock expire together, giving early participants a clear exit window.

**Interface:**

```solidity
interface IVotingEscrow {
    /// @notice Lock WOOD tokens for veWOOD fee share weight
    /// @param amount Amount of WOOD to lock
    /// @param duration Lock duration in seconds (min 4 weeks, max 1 year)
    /// @return tokenId The veNFT token ID
    function createLock(uint256 amount, uint256 duration) external returns (uint256 tokenId);

    /// @notice Add more WOOD to an existing lock
    function increaseAmount(uint256 tokenId, uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Extend lock duration
    function increaseUnlockTime(uint256 tokenId, uint256 newDuration) external;

    /// @notice Enable/disable auto-max-lock
    function setAutoMaxLock(uint256 tokenId, bool enabled) external;

    /// @notice Withdraw WOOD after lock expires
    function withdraw(uint256 tokenId) external;

    /// @notice Get current fee share weight for a veNFT
    function balanceOfNFT(uint256 tokenId) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Get fee share weight at a past timestamp
    function balanceOfNFTAt(uint256 tokenId, uint256 timestamp) external view returns (uint256);
}
```

### 2. Revenue Sharing (FeeDistributor.sol)

**This is the core mechanism replacing emissions.** veWOOD holders earn real protocol fee revenue, paid in the vault's underlying asset (USDC, WETH, etc.), not in WOOD tokens.

**Source:** Protocol fees collected via `protocolFeeBps` on strategy settlement in `SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()`.

**Distribution each epoch (7 days):**

| Recipient | Share | Asset | Purpose |
|-----------|-------|-------|---------|
| veWOOD holders | 60% | USDC | Protocol fee share in USDC — pro-rata by fee share weight |
| Buyback & lock | 20% | Vault asset → WOOD → veWOOD | Supply reduction, protocol-owned veWOOD |
| Protocol treasury | 20% | Vault asset | Operations, development, audits (multisig-controlled) |

**Epoch:** 7-day period, Thursday 00:00 UTC → Wednesday 23:59 UTC (standard).

**Claim mechanism:** veWOOD holders claim their fee share after each epoch. Claims are computed on-chain using VotingEscrow checkpoints — no Merkle trees or off-chain infrastructure.

```solidity
interface IFeeDistributor {
    /// @notice Deposit USDC protocol fees for distribution in the current epoch
    /// @param amount The USDC amount to deposit
    /// @dev Only accepts USDC. Non-USDC fees must be converted off-chain before deposit.
    ///      Callable by multisig or authorized keeper after off-chain conversion.
    function depositFees(uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Claim earned USDC fees for a veNFT across one or more epochs
    /// @param tokenId The veNFT to claim for
    /// @param epochs Array of epoch numbers to claim
    function claim(uint256 tokenId, uint256[] calldata epochs) external;

    /// @notice Claim all pending USDC fees across all epochs
    /// @param tokenId The veNFT to claim for
    function claimAll(uint256 tokenId) external;

    /// @notice View pending USDC rewards for a veNFT
    function pendingRewards(uint256 tokenId, uint256 epoch) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Get total USDC deposited for an epoch
    function epochFees(uint256 epoch) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Execute the epoch fee distribution split
    /// @dev Called at epoch flip — splits deposited USDC into veWOOD/buyback/treasury buckets.
    ///      Permissionless but reverts if epoch hasn't ended or no fees deposited.
    ///      Rounding dust (from integer division across 3 buckets) accumulates in a
    ///      sweep-able dust buffer, claimable by treasury via sweepDust().
    function distributeEpoch(uint256 epoch) external;
}
```

**Fee calculation:**

```solidity
function _calculateReward(uint256 tokenId, uint256 epoch) internal view returns (uint256) {
    uint256 epochTimestamp = epochBoundary(epoch);
    uint256 holderWeight = votingEscrow.balanceOfNFTAt(tokenId, epochTimestamp);
    uint256 totalWeight = votingEscrow.totalSupplyAt(epochTimestamp);
    if (totalWeight == 0) return 0;

    uint256 veWOODShare = epochFees[epoch] * VEWOOD_FEE_SHARE / DENOMINATOR; // 60%
    return veWOODShare * holderWeight / totalWeight;
}
```

**Claim window:** Fees are claimable for 52 epochs (1 year). Unclaimed fees after expiry are returned to the protocol treasury.

**Single-token settlement (USDC).** FeeDistributor is a pure USDC-in/USDC-out contract — it accepts only USDC deposits, with no on-chain swap logic. This eliminates MEV/sandwich attack risk that would exist if the contract swapped tokens on-chain via a DEX router.

**Fee conversion flow (off-chain, MEV-free):**

```
Strategy settles → SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()
  → Protocol fee share sent to FeeStaging multisig address (in original token: WETH, USDC, etc.)
  → Multisig converts non-USDC tokens to USDC off-chain via CoW Protocol (batch auction, MEV-resistant)
  → Multisig calls FeeDistributor.depositFees(usdcAmount) with the converted USDC
```

The conversion is an **operational step, not a smart contract concern.** This is simpler and safer — FeeDistributor doesn't need swap logic, router approvals, or slippage handling. The multisig (or an authorized keeper) handles conversion at their discretion using CoW's MEV-protected batch auctions.

veWOOD holders always claim in USDC regardless of which vault asset generated the fees.

### 3. Bootstrapping Incentives (BootstrapRewards.sol)

**Replaces the open-ended emission model.** 75M WOOD (15% of supply) are allocated as time-limited bootstrapping rewards. These are **non-transferable for the first 6 months** (Morpho-style), meaning recipients accumulate fee share weight without sell pressure.

**Distribution schedule (12 months, steep decay):**

| Period | WOOD Distributed | % of Budget | Monthly Rate |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|--------------|
| Month 1-3 | 30M | 40% | 10M/month |
| Month 4-6 | 22.5M | 30% | 7.5M/month |
| Month 7-9 | 15M | 20% | 5M/month |
| Month 10-12 | 7.5M | 10% | 2.5M/month |
| Month 13+ | 0 | — | Protocol runs on revenue |

**Recipient allocation per epoch:**

| Recipient | Share | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Vault depositors | 55% | Bootstrap TVL — the primary growth lever |
| veWOOD lockers | 30% | Reward early fee-sharing participants |
| shareToken/WOOD LPs | 15% | Bootstrap pool depth for secondary market exit |

**Non-transferable mechanics:**

```solidity
interface IBootstrapRewards {
    /// @notice Claim earned bootstrapping rewards for vault depositors (55% bucket)
    /// @param vault The vault deposited into — rewards pro-rata by time-weighted deposit size
    function claimDepositorRewards(address vault) external;

    /// @notice Claim earned bootstrapping rewards for veWOOD lockers (30% bucket)
    /// @dev Pro-rata by veWOOD fee share weight at epoch boundary
    function claimLockerRewards() external;

    /// @notice Claim earned bootstrapping rewards for shareToken/WOOD LPs (15% bucket, months 1-6)
    /// @param pool The Aerodrome pool address — rewards pro-rata by LP share
    function claimLPRewards(address pool) external;

    /// @notice Check if tokens are transferable yet
    function isTransferable() external view returns (bool);

    /// @notice Get total earned rewards for an account (across all buckets)
    function earned(address account) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Get claimable (vested + unlocked) rewards
    function claimable(address account) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice Convert non-transferable rewards to transferable WOOD (partial or full)
    /// @param amount Amount to convert (pass type(uint256).max for all)
    /// @dev Only callable after the 6-month unlock date
    function convertToTransferable(uint256 amount) external;

    /// @notice Lock non-transferable rewards directly into veWOOD
    /// @dev Callable before the 6-month unlock — enables fee sharing without sell pressure
    function lockIntoVeWOOD(uint256 amount, uint256 duration) external returns (uint256 tokenId);
}
```

**Reward allocation within buckets:**
- **Depositors (55%):** Pro-rata by time-weighted deposit size within each vault, computed at epoch boundary. Uses the same snapshot checkpoint system as FeeDistributor.
- **veWOOD lockers (30%):** Pro-rata by veWOOD fee share weight at epoch boundary. Same weight used for fee distribution.
- **LPs (15%, months 1-6 only):** Pro-rata by LP token balance in protocol-tracked Aerodrome pools.

**Implementation:** BootstrapRewards holds the 75M WOOD allocation. Claimed rewards are tracked as internal balances (not ERC-20 transfers). After 6 months, holders call `convertToTransferable()` to receive actual WOOD tokens. During the non-transferable period, rewards can be locked into veWOOD via `lockIntoVeWOOD()` — providing fee share weight without any sell pressure.

**Why non-transferable?** This is the critical innovation borrowed from Morpho. During months 1-6, bootstrapping rewards create alignment (recipients can lock for veWOOD and earn fees) without any sell pressure on the WOOD market. By month 6, the protocol should have established revenue, making the token's value grounded in fundamentals before the bootstrapping supply enters circulation.

**LP incentives (months 1-6):** 15% of bootstrapping rewards go to shareToken/WOOD LPs. This solves the cold-start liquidity problem — depositors need pool depth to exit, but LPs need volume to justify providing liquidity. After month 6, LP incentives end and pools are sustained by protocol-owned liquidity + organic trading fees.

**After month 12: zero emissions.** The protocol must sustain itself entirely on real fee revenue. If revenue is insufficient to attract participants, that's a signal to improve the product — not to print more tokens.

### 4. Aerodrome Pools (shareToken/WOOD)

Each syndicate vault produces share tokens (e.g., `swUSDC`, `swETH`). Per-syndicate Aerodrome Slipstream pools serve as secondary markets:

**Purpose (without gauges — gauges removed):**
1. **Early exit for depositors.** Vault redemptions are locked during active proposals (`redemptionsLocked()`). The shareToken/WOOD pool provides a secondary market where depositors can sell their share tokens without waiting for strategy settlement.
2. **Organic WOOD demand.** Depositors sell shareTokens → get WOOD → sell WOOD on WOOD/WETH → exit to stables. This creates natural buy-side demand for WOOD.
3. **Price discovery.** ShareTokens are equity-like claims on syndicate performance. The pool price reflects the market's assessment of a syndicate's strategy quality.

**Note:** These pools exist purely for depositor exit liquidity and price discovery. Pool creation is **permissionless** — anyone can create a pool for any syndicate.

**Primary WOOD/WETH pool (one-time):**

A deep WOOD/WETH pool on Aerodrome Slipstream serves as the canonical market for WOOD price discovery. We seed it directly at TGE — no public sale or LBP — using the two-sided + single-sided Clanker-style approach:

1. Call `CLFactory.createPool(WOOD, WETH, tickSpacing, sqrtPriceX96)` — tick spacing 100 (≈0.3% fee equivalent)
2. Seed two-sided concentrated position around the launch price: protocol-paired WOOD + ~5 ETH from treasury via `NonfungiblePositionManager.mint()`
3. Seed single-sided WOOD-only position above launch price — acts as a permanent limit-sell ladder (Clanker-style), price-discovery anchor, and protocol revenue source
4. Combined deployment: ~90M WOOD + ~5 ETH from the 150M POL allocation
5. Position NFTs held by protocol multisig — locked, no active rebalancing
6. Optionally request an Aerodrome gauge to earn AERO rewards on protocol-owned LP

**Per-syndicate shareToken/WOOD pools:**

1. **Create pool:** Call `CLFactory.createPool(shareToken, WOOD, tickSpacing, sqrtPriceX96)` — **permissionless**, anyone can do this
3. **Seed liquidity (single-sided WOOD):**
   - Protocol seeds WOOD-only into a tick range above the current price for the first N syndicates (genesis cohort, e.g. first 10)
   - Total budget: ~60M WOOD across the genesis cohort, drawn from the 150M POL allocation
   - Pool becomes two-sided as depositors sell shareTokens for early exit
4. **Self-bootstrap:** After the genesis cohort, new syndicates create their own pools permissionlessly. Syndicate agents or community members can seed liquidity. Per-syndicate LP rewards or depositor exit incentives, when needed, are funded from the **Community / grants (15%)** bucket — not a separate token allocation.

**Bootstrapping via early exit demand:**

```
1. Protocol seeds WOOD into pool (tick range above current price)
2. Depositor holds shareTokens but vault redemptions are locked
3. Depositor sells shareTokens into pool → gets WOOD
4. Pool absorbs shareTokens, releases WOOD → price moves into range
5. Depositor sells WOOD on WOOD/WETH pair → exits to WETH/stables
6. Pool is now two-sided, organic trading continues
```

This is not a fallback mechanism — it is the primary bootstrapping approach. The protocol only needs to provide WOOD; depositors bring the shareToken side through natural early-exit demand.

**Protocol-owned position management:**
- **Range selection:** Set tick range 20-50% above the initial shareToken/WOOD price. Wide enough to absorb early trades without going out of range.
- **Rebalancing:** No active rebalancing. If price moves significantly, the multisig can create a new position at the current price using treasury WOOD.
- **Owned by:** Protocol multisig. Position NFTs held by multisig directly.

**Fee capture:**
- Protocol-owned LP position fees accumulate in positions
- Multisig collects fees periodically and sends to protocol treasury
- Treasury uses accumulated fees to seed shareToken/WOOD pools for new syndicates — makes pool creation self-sustaining
- Community LPs keep their own swap fees

**Fee tier:** 1% (10000) for less liquid shareToken/WOOD pairs, 0.3% (3000) for higher-volume pairs. Configurable per pool.

**Pool depth sustainability (post-bootstrapping):**

After LP bootstrapping incentives end at month 6, pool depth is sustained by:

1. **Protocol-Owned Liquidity (POL).** Genesis-seeded LP positions remain permanently. Treasury can deepen positions for high-activity pools using collected fees.
2. **NAV arbitrage.** Each shareToken has intrinsic value (vault NAV per share). Price divergence from NAV attracts arbitrageurs, creating recurring two-sided volume.
3. **Speculative trading on syndicate performance.** Traders buy shareTokens anticipating strong upcoming proposals or wanting exposure to a syndicate's track record.
4. **Buyback as recurring buy pressure.** The buyback mechanism purchases WOOD on WOOD/WETH using 20% of fee revenue every epoch, creating consistent buy pressure that propagates to shareToken/WOOD pools via cross-pool arbitrage.
5. **AERO gauge rewards.** If the WOOD/WETH pool receives an Aerodrome gauge, protocol-owned LP positions earn AERO rewards — additional revenue for the treasury independent of WOOD trading fees.

### 5. Buyback-and-Lock (BuybackEngine.sol)

**Retained from prior design with modifications.** 20% of protocol fee revenue is used to buy WOOD from the WOOD/WETH pool and lock it as protocol-owned veWOOD.

**Execution via CoW Protocol TWAP:**

```solidity
interface IBuybackEngine {
    /// @notice Execute a buyback using accumulated USDC from FeeDistributor
    /// @param amount The USDC amount to sell for WOOD
    /// @param minWoodOut Minimum WOOD to receive (slippage protection via 30-min TWAP)
    /// @dev Creates a CoW TWAP order: 24 parts, 1hr intervals. USDC-only — matches FeeDistributor.
    function executeBuyback(uint256 amount, uint256 minWoodOut) external;

    /// @notice Lock purchased WOOD as protocol veWOOD (auto-max-lock)
    /// @dev Called by CoW post-hook after TWAP order fills
    function lockPurchasedWood() external;

    /// @notice View accumulated fee revenue pending buyback
    function pendingBuyback(address token) external view returns (uint256);

    /// @notice View total WOOD bought and locked historically
    function totalBoughtAndLocked() external view returns (uint256);
}
```

**Why lock instead of burn:** Locked WOOD earns fee revenue share — the protocol treasury becomes a productive participant that accumulates protocol fees. This also avoids regulatory concerns around token burning (which some jurisdictions view as evidence of securities-like properties).

**CoW Protocol is live on Base** (confirmed — actively settling trades with solver competition). No fallback needed. If CoW experiences downtime, buybacks simply queue and execute once service resumes.

**Impact modeling:**

| Annual Protocol Revenue | 20% to Buyback | At $0.05 WOOD | Annual WOOD Removed |
|------------------------|----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| $500K | $100K | $0.05 | 2M WOOD (0.4% of supply) |
| $2M | $400K | $0.05 | 8M WOOD (1.6% of supply) |
| $10M | $2M | $0.05 | 40M WOOD (8% of supply) |
| $50M | $10M | $0.05 | 200M WOOD (40% of supply) |

At scale, the buyback becomes a significant supply sink — but only if the protocol generates real revenue.

## Protocol Control

**All protocol operations are controlled by a 3-of-5 protocol multisig.** There is no on-chain governance via veWOOD. veWOOD is purely an economic mechanism for fee sharing.

A separate **2-of-3 pause guardian multisig** can halt contracts in emergencies (faster response than 3-of-5). Unpausing requires the full 3-of-5 protocol multisig. The two multisigs share some but not all signers.

**Protocol multisig responsibilities:**
- Protocol parameter changes (fee rates, bootstrapping schedule adjustments)
- Treasury spending
- Emergency actions (pause contracts, adjust parameters)
- Pool management (seeding new shareToken/WOOD pools, collecting LP fees)
- Fee distribution ratio changes (if ever needed)
- Contract upgrades / migrations

**Why no governance?** Shipping fast matters more than governance theater. On-chain governance adds contract complexity, attack surface, and delays. A multisig is simpler, faster to deploy, and honest about the current trust model. If the community demands on-chain governance as the protocol matures, it can be added in a future phase — the VotingEscrow contract already tracks fee share weight that could double as voting power.

## Fee Architecture Integration

The current design simplifies the fee architecture. Two revenue streams, clean separation:

### 1. Strategy Profits (vault asset) — existing, unchanged

> Source: `SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()`

```
Gross Profit (from strategy settlement)
  ├─ Protocol Fee (protocolFeeBps) → FeeDistributor.sol
  ├─ Agent Fee (bps) → proposer + co-proposers
  ├─ Management Fee (bps) → vault owner
  └─ Remainder → vault (depositors)
```

### 2. Fee Distribution (new — replaces emissions)

```
SyndicateGovernor._distributeFees()
  → Protocol fee share → FeeStaging multisig (in original vault asset)
  → Off-chain conversion to USDC via CoW Protocol (MEV-free)
  → FeeDistributor.depositFees(usdcAmount)

FeeDistributor (each epoch)
  ├─ 60% → veWOOD holders (claim in USDC)
  ├─ 20% → BuybackEngine (CoW TWAP → WOOD → veWOOD)
  └─ 20% → Protocol treasury multisig
```

### 3. LP Fee Revenue (protocol-owned positions)

```
Aerodrome protocol-owned LP positions (WOOD/WETH + shareToken/WOOD)
  → Multisig collects periodically
  → Protocol treasury (seeds new syndicate pools)
```

**Who earns what:**

| Participant | Vault Asset Profits | Fee Revenue Share | Bootstrapping WOOD | LP Fees |
|-------------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Vault depositors | Remainder after fees | — | 55% (months 1-12) | — |
| Vault owner | Management fee | — | — | — |
| Agent (proposer) | Performance fee | — | — | — |
| veWOOD holders | — | 60% (protocol fee share in USDC) | 30% (months 1-12) | — |
| shareToken/WOOD LPs | — | — | 15% (months 1-6) | Swap fees (kept) |
| Protocol treasury | Protocol fee → FeeDistributor | 20% | — | Protocol-owned position fees |
| Buyback engine | — | 20% (→ locked veWOOD) | — | — |

**Comparison to v3:** v3 had four distinct revenue streams (strategy profits, WOOD emissions, trading fees, bribes) flowing through multiple contracts (Minter, FeeCollector, VoteIncentive, VaultRewardsDistributor, RewardsDistributor). This consolidates to two: strategy profits (existing) and fee distribution (new), plus LP fees from protocol-owned positions managed by multisig. This reduces contract surface area from 9 to 5.

### Pool Creation Flow

**Primary WOOD/WETH pool (one-time, Aero Launch — no public sale):**
1. Call `CLFactory.createPool(WOOD, WETH, tickSpacing, sqrtPriceX96)` — tick spacing 100 (≈0.3% fee equivalent)
2. Seed two-sided concentrated position at launch price: protocol-paired WOOD + ~5 ETH from treasury via `NonfungiblePositionManager.mint()`
3. Seed single-sided WOOD-only position above launch price — Clanker-style limit-sell ladder
4. Combined: ~90M WOOD + ~5 ETH from the 150M POL allocation. Remaining ~60M WOOD reserved for per-syndicate pool seeding
5. Position NFTs held by protocol multisig — locked
6. Optionally request a gauge from Aerodrome governance to earn AERO rewards on protocol-owned LP

**Per-syndicate shareToken/WOOD pools:**
1. **Create pool:** Call `CLFactory.createPool(shareToken, WOOD, tickSpacing, sqrtPriceX96)` — permissionless
2. **Seed liquidity (single-sided WOOD):**
   - Calculate tick range above current price (pool acts as limit sell for WOOD)
   - Call `NonfungiblePositionManager.mint()` with WOOD only
   - Pool becomes two-sided as depositors sell shareTokens for early exit
3. **Budget:** ~60M WOOD across the genesis cohort (drawn from the 150M POL allocation). Per-syndicate LP rewards or depositor exit incentives, when needed, are funded from the **Community / grants (15%)** bucket — not a separate token allocation.

## Epoch Lifecycle

The epoch lifecycle is significantly simplified from v3. No gauge voting, no emission minting, no rebase calculation, no governance votes.

```
Thursday 00:00 UTC — Epoch N starts
│
├── Multisig deposits converted USDC into FeeDistributor (if not already done)
│
├── FeeDistributor.distributeEpoch(N-1)
│   ├── Split deposited USDC: 60% veWOOD / 20% buyback / 20% treasury
│   ├── Transfer treasury share to multisig
│   └── Queue buyback share for BuybackEngine
│
├── BuybackEngine.executeBuyback()
│   ├── Create CoW TWAP order (24 parts, 1hr intervals)
│   └── Post-hook: lock purchased WOOD as veWOOD (auto-max-lock)
│
├── BootstrapRewards.updateEpoch() [months 1-12 only]
│   ├── Calculate epoch's reward budget (from decay schedule)
│   └── Make rewards claimable for depositors/lockers/LPs
│
├── veWOOD holders claim epoch N-1 fee rewards (in USDC)
├── Vault depositors claim epoch N-1 bootstrapping rewards [months 1-12]
├── LPs claim bootstrapping rewards [months 1-6]
│
Wednesday 23:59 UTC — Epoch N ends
```

**Permissionless epoch flip:** `distributeEpoch()` is callable by anyone after the epoch boundary. A keeper bot or any user can trigger it. Small gas refund incentivizes timely execution.

## Security Considerations

1. **Reentrancy:** VotingEscrow handles NFTs and token transfers — use ReentrancyGuard on all external calls.

2. **Flash loan attacks:** Fee share weight is based on locked balance (not transferable), immune to flash loans.

3. **Checkpoint manipulation:** Use `block.timestamp` checkpoints for weight snapshots. FeeDistributor must snapshot veWOOD balances at epoch boundary, not at claim time.

4. **Oracle manipulation:** WOOD/WETH pool price can be manipulated via flash loans. **Never use spot pool price on-chain.** BuybackEngine uses a 30-minute TWAP via Aerodrome Slipstream's `observe()`.

5. **Fee token handling:** FeeDistributor accepts only USDC — no on-chain swap logic, no router approvals, no MEV/sandwich risk. Non-USDC fee conversion happens off-chain via CoW Protocol (MEV-protected batch auctions) before deposit. FeeDistributor uses SafeERC20 for USDC transfers. Access control: `depositFees()` callable only by multisig or authorized keeper to prevent griefing.

6. **BuybackEngine MEV protection:** CoW TWAP orders are MEV-resistant by design (batch auction settlement). The `minWoodOut` parameter provides additional slippage protection.

7. **Non-transferable token edge cases:** BootstrapRewards non-transferable balances must not be usable as collateral, transferable via approval, or flashable. Internal accounting only — no ERC-20 balance until `convertToTransferable()` is called after 6 months.

8. **Access control:** All pausable contracts use a 2-of-3 multisig as pause guardian. Unpausing requires a 3-of-5 protocol multisig to prevent premature restart.

9. **Upgradeability:** All WOOD contracts are deployed as **immutable** (not UUPS upgradeable). VotingEscrow holds locked tokens — upgradeability would introduce rug risk. Migration path for bugs: (a) pause affected contract, (b) deploy new contract, (c) snapshot state, (d) users migrate via claim mechanism.

10. **Overflow:** veWOOD fee share weight calculation uses time math — careful with uint256 overflow at boundaries. FeeDistributor division must handle rounding correctly (round down, accumulate dust in a sweep-able buffer).

11. **Pool manipulation:** shareToken/WOOD pools are thin markets, especially early. Large trades can move price significantly. This is acceptable — these pools exist for exit liquidity, not as price oracles. Never use shareToken/WOOD pool prices for on-chain calculations.

12. **Aerodrome dependency:** Protocol-owned liquidity sits on Aerodrome. If Aerodrome experiences a critical vulnerability, LP positions could be at risk. Mitigation: monitor Aerodrome security disclosures, maintain ability to withdraw positions via multisig. Aerodrome is battle-tested with >$1B TVL.

## Regulatory Considerations

> **This section is not legal advice. It identifies risks and recommends actions.**

### Howey Test Analysis (US Securities Law)

| Prong | Assessment | Risk |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Investment of money** | Met (buy WOOD, lock it) | Met |
| **Common enterprise** | Likely met (token value tied to protocol) | Likely met |
| **Expectation of profits** | Arguably met (explicit fee revenue share) | ⚠ Higher risk than indirect models |
| **Solely from efforts of others** | **Key factor:** No governance — holders are passive recipients of fee revenue. Multisig controls everything. | ⚠ Weak defense without governance |

**Regulatory trade-offs:**

The explicit revenue sharing model (60% of fees to veWOOD) is transparent but dividend-like. Without governance duties for veWOOD holders, the "not solely from efforts of others" defense is weaker than if holders had active governance responsibilities.

- **Pro:** Honest framing. No obfuscation through complex emission/bribe/governance mechanics.
- **Con:** Regulators may view direct fee distribution to passive holders as strong evidence of a security.
- **Future mitigation:** If regulatory pressure demands it, on-chain governance can be added to veWOOD (the VotingEscrow already tracks weight that could serve as voting power). This would strengthen the "active participation" defense.

**Improvements over v3:**
- No inflationary emissions (removes "profiting from token appreciation driven by artificial demand")
- Non-transferable bootstrapping rewards reduce "investment contract" framing
- No bribe layer removes the optics of paying for token direction
- Simpler model is easier to explain to regulators

### Recommendations

1. **Engage securities counsel** before TGE — the fee revenue share model needs explicit legal sign-off
2. **No geographic restrictions needed for token launch** under Aero Launch (no public sale tranche to gate). Standard secondary-market disclaimers still apply for the WOOD/WETH pool
3. **Utility-first messaging** — position WOOD as a protocol fee-sharing token, not a yield-bearing investment
4. **Avoid profit language** — use "protocol fee share", not "yield", "dividends", "returns"
5. **SAFT structure** for any pre-sale allocations
6. **Consider adding governance later** — if regulatory analysis recommends it, veWOOD governance would strengthen the utility/participation defense

## Downside Scenario Planning

### Scenario 1: Low Protocol Revenue (< $100K/year)

**What breaks:** veWOOD fee share is negligible. At $100K annual revenue and 60% to veWOOD holders, total veWOOD yield is $60K/year. If 100M WOOD is locked ($5M at $0.05/WOOD), that's 1.2% APR — not attractive enough to lock.

**Why this is different from v3:** In v3, low revenue didn't matter because emissions provided artificial yield. In v4, low revenue means low token utility — period. This is by design. The token SHOULD have low value if the protocol doesn't generate revenue.

**Responses:**
1. **Bootstrapping incentives (months 1-12)** provide supplemental yield during the revenue ramp period
2. **Focus on product** — improve strategy performance, onboard more agents, grow TVL. The token's job is to reflect protocol value, not create it.
3. **Treasury runway:** 75M WOOD treasury (15%) + 25M reserve (5%) provides 18+ months of operational funding even with zero fee revenue

### Scenario 2: WOOD Price Decline (>50%)

**What breaks:** Protocol-owned liquidity positions lose value. Bootstrapping WOOD rewards are worth less.

**Responses:**
1. **No emission-based circuit breaker needed** — there are no emissions to cut. The protocol's cost structure is fixed (team vesting is predetermined, bootstrapping schedule is predetermined)
2. **Buyback becomes more efficient** — 20% of fee revenue buys more WOOD at lower prices, creating counter-cyclical buy pressure
3. **Fee revenue is in USDC** — veWOOD holders earn protocol fee share regardless of WOOD price. This actually stabilizes the model vs v3 where falling WOOD price made emissions worthless.

### Scenario 3: Bootstrap Period Ends, Revenue Insufficient

**What breaks:** Month 13 arrives, bootstrapping incentives end, but protocol revenue isn't enough to sustain veWOOD yields that attract locking.

**Responses:**
1. **This is the honest outcome.** If the protocol can't generate enough revenue in 12 months to sustain its token economy, it should not be propped up by inflation.
2. **Multisig can allocate treasury/reserve WOOD** for an extended bootstrapping period — but this is a conscious decision, not an automatic mechanism.
3. **Minimum viable state:** Even with zero fee revenue, the protocol still functions. Vaults still operate. Strategies still execute. Agents still earn performance fees. The token just doesn't have yield — which is honest pricing.

### Scenario 4: Low LP Depth After Bootstrapping

**What breaks:** shareToken/WOOD pool depth drops after LP incentives end at month 6. Depositors can't exit efficiently during locked redemptions.

**Responses:**
1. **Protocol-owned liquidity remains.** Genesis-seeded positions are permanent. Treasury can deepen positions using collected fees.
2. **NAV arbitrage sustains volume.** ShareToken/vault NAV divergence creates recurring arbitrage flow.
3. **Depositors can wait for redemption unlock.** Pools are a convenience, not a requirement — vault redemptions unlock when strategies settle.
4. **If critical:** Multisig can allocate treasury WOOD to deepen specific pools.
